Problem Statement

Users start with intent but drop when planning and tracking become fragmented. The product needed a unified daily flow that makes workouts, meal choices, and progress tracking feel connected.

Pain point: users lose continuity between workout and nutrition planning.
Pain point: progress metrics are often noisy and non-actionable.
UX goal: reduce cognitive load and guide the next best action.
UX goal: make repeat usage fast enough for daily routine behavior.

User Personas

Two distinct users shaped every product and architecture decision.

Ms. Anita Sharma, 32 — Working Mom
Occupation: Marketing professional, mother of one
Goal: exercise 10–30 min daily, manage diet, build routine without disrupting family life
Pain point: struggles to find time; gets distracted and misses sessions
Needs: guided fitness plan, nutrition management, habit tracking, and daily motivation
Mr. Rohan Patil, 27 — Fitness Enthusiast
Occupation: IT professional with an active social life
Goal: track detailed metrics, build physique, join social fitness challenges
Pain point: plateauing; struggles to stay motivated without accountability
Needs: advanced metric tracking, social challenges, competition features, and goal streaks

Research Phase

Five "How Might We" questions framed the entire product direction and feature prioritisation.

HMW 1

Turn complex biometrics into a simple, intuitive 360° body visual?

HMW 2

Create micro-workouts that keep users consistent with just 15 minutes a day?

HMW 3

Make fitness a social, accountability-driven experience?

HMW 4

Use AI to auto-log nutrition from a single photo or quick description?

HMW 5

Build a frictionless daily Metric Hub that encourages effortless progress tracking?

User Journey

Discovery and Onboarding

User sees ad, evaluates value prop against competing apps
Insight: "Is this actually effective?"
Design response: zero-friction setup, immediate clarity

Activity Selection

User browses workout library or chooses a pre-built plan
Insight: "Which workout should I start with?"
Design response: AI recommendations + a "let's go" coach nudge

Tracking and Execution

User works out and logs metrics in real time
Insight: "Am I improving? How much longer?"
Design response: live feedback, offline mode, motivating cues

Retention and Community

User views progress, shares milestones, joins challenges
Feeling: "Making progress — challenges motivate me!"
Design response: mindfulness rewards, streaks, social challenge loops

Product Map and Architecture

Four core navigation nodes, each mapped to distinct user intent states.

Home and Track

Daily dashboard with progress snapshots
AI-driven daily nudge and metric quick-view
Calorie tracker and workout-in-progress state
Attach workouts and metric logs

Plan and Profile

Daily planner, core program, discovery feed
Content library with full customisation
Personal branding and social growth
Physical metrics, fitness goals, system settings
