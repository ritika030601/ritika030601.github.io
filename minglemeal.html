UX Case Study - Food Rescue Network

MingleMeal

MingleMeal is a food rescue platform connecting restaurants, volunteers, and NGOs to reduce food wastage. This is a UX-focused case study covering research, the donor journey, system mapping, and role-based product flows. Screens shown are mid-fidelity wireframes.

Problem Statement

Food surplus and hunger coexist because coordination between donors, transport, and receiving organizations is fragmented. The system needed a reliable, low-friction donation journey that all actors can complete quickly.

Donation process lacked one accountable, end-to-end path.
Time-sensitive pickups failed due to delayed assignment flow.
Role interfaces showed too much information at once.
Visibility gaps reduced trust across stakeholders.

Research and Market Context

The opportunity was grounded in data: India generates ~74 million tonnes of food waste annually, and platforms for donation coordination were either absent or too fragmented to trust.

Why MingleMeal

Annual food wastage in India costs the economy ₹90,000 crore
Global targets aim to reduce per-capita food waste by half by 2030
No real-time app existed to manage surplus donation digitally
Identified root causes behind donation drop-offs at each stage

Deep Dive Findings

Donor journey lacked clarity at posting and confirmation
Volunteer assignment needed stronger context and validation
NGOs required faster verification and live pickup visibility
Urgency points where delay directly leads to wastage were unmapped

Deep Dive — Research and Insight

Primary research conducted across three stakeholder groups to map real operational needs and friction points.

Restaurants — The Donors

Why food is wasted: over-estimation of demand and order cancellations
Food tracking method: mostly manual checks; no formal digital log
Best pickup time: 30 minutes before or after closing
Info per donation: dish name, approximate weight, pickup time

NGOs — The Receivers

Daily capacity: 50–300 meals depending on storage
Beneficiary tracking: manual registers at distribution centers
Biggest digital need: real-time alerts for available food nearby
Cannot store overnight — no industrial fridges; need immediate dispatch

Drivers — The Bridge

Most have two-wheelers; limited access to large vans
Available 1–5 hours on weekends or post-work weekdays
Need exact food weight and Google Maps location before a task
Stay active for: certificates, digital badges, fuel reimbursements

Donor Journey

Mapped four key stages, capturing what users do, what they feel, key touchpoints, and ideas for improvement.

Awareness and Initiation

User signs up and asks: "Is this legal / safe?"
Logs food type, quantity, and available pickup time
Improvement: add clear legal/liability disclaimer up front
Improvement: use AI to suggest optimised pickup times

Connect and Track Impact

Push notification to nearby NGO/Volunteer
User wonders: "When will someone pick it up?"
Views metrics on meals provided and CO₂ saved
Improvement: integrate WhatsApp for direct coordination

Role-Based Product Flows

Restaurant Login

Registers with business credentials via dashboard
Selects food type, passes AI freshness check
Sets pickup location and time, reviews and posts
Alert triggers to nearby NGOs for collection

Driver / Volunteer Login

Registers with personal and vehicle details
Sees task list of food packages; accepts a task
Navigates to restaurant, scans QR code to confirm pickup
Follows route to NGO, scans QR again at drop-off

NGO Login

Registers with legal credentials to access Food Map
Receives real-time donation alerts; reviews food details
Clicks "Claim" to track delivery via live map
Scans driver QR on arrival to confirm receipt and record impact

System Map Logic

All three roles share a unified status language
States: pending → accepted → assigned → in-transit → confirmed
QR codes provide verification at every handoff point
Badges and fuel reimbursement reward volunteer service
